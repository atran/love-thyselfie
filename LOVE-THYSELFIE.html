RENDER WANTS YOUR SELFIES

LOVE THYSELFIE is a weekly RENDER installment that seeks to reappropriate and reclaim the act and intent behind taking personal webcam photos. The shame-like stigma surrounding the ubiquitous webcam ‘selfie’— a concept unique to and mastered by the millennial generation—is a reflection of how the phenomena of contemporary youth culture are both overexposed and over-sensationalized.

We live in an era with unprecedented forms of identity-making: kids are growing up without being able to imagine a world without computers or the Internet. A generation of digital natives, today’s youth are armed with an intense and hyperaccelerated desire to shape cyber identities and forge socially networked trajectories in an increasingly digitized world.

We often wonder: are we truly as narcissistic as the media claims? Or are we simply the first generation to be granted a guaranteed and massive public sphere to broadcast ourselves?

This “narcissism epidemic” is neither a novel concept nor a cultivated detriment, but only a manifestation of the natural human instinct to seek approval and validation from one’s peers.

In an effort to provide some necessary context to the claim that millennials are inherently narcissistic, RENDER aims to provide you with both a platform and an outlet to express yourself freely without shame or judgement.

Through LOVE THYSELFIE, we hope to take the idea of hyper-narcissism and radically turn it on its head. It’s not a celebration of self-obsession, but rather an acknowledgement and exploration of narcissism as an arbitrary yet universal personality trait that has been inaccurately and stigmatically designated to our kind.
